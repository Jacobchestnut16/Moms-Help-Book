Federal Government Payer Program
Medicare Certification
Beneficiaries of Medicare are unable to access healthcare services at chosen facilities if it
has not authorized by Medicare. Facilities that have received certification by Medicare tend to
perform at higher standards than those who are not. Medicare requirements for accreditation
align with regulation standards set by the Department of Health and Human Services (DHHS).
Without certification, payment for services will not be processed. In conclusion if facilities lack
Medicare certification there is a major possibility that the delivery of healthcare will affect the
quality and frequency of care.
Accreditation and Licensing
Knowing the differences is crucial for healthcare workers who may be aiming for a
specific therapeutic area or training. Accreditation can be awarded by entities, organizations, or
associations not affiliated with any government[ CITATION SCI20 \l 1033 ]. Licensure is about
getting the legal right to practice or serve in a specific role[ CITATION SCI20 \l 1033 ].
Quality Reporting System and Reimbursement
Quality reporting systems focus on improving quality of healthcare through measurement
and transparency. The program has been changed several times but continues to financially
reward hospitals for reporting data about quality that is then made available to the public (Oachs
& Watters, 2020). These reporting systems must deliberate and careful when created as to collect
all the relevant data for quality comparisons.
Reimbursement services aim to incentivize or bribe providers to perform only high-
quality services. Overall patient care is superior when there is a set standard based on the

Patient Medicare Eligibility Requirements
The average age for Medicare eligibility is 65 in which most individuals are auto
enrolled. Medicare provides fixed costs for standard of care services such as vital signs and
physical exams. Depending on which benefits individuals have registered to, there are numerous
times that out-of-pocket costs are accrued. They estimate that even in the apparent absence of
health benefits, the insurance value of Medicare alone is enough to cover between 45 percent and
75 percent of its costs [ CITATION Mat06 \l 1033 ]. With the increased cost considered, imagine
if an individual suffers a cerebrovascular accident, what costs will be associated with treatment
and maintenance of the condition? A handful may deem Medicare too restrictive for the
population that use Medicare.
Federal Legislation
The Patient Safety and Quality Improvement Act of 2005 (PSQIA) establishes a
voluntary reporting system designed to enhance the data available to assess and resolve patient
safety and health care quality issues [ CITATION Pat17 \l 1033 ]. The PSQIA affects healthcare
in many ways such as provider communication, data accuracy, appropriate usage of antibiotics,
etc. Overall, the act strives to improve patient safety and implementation within hospitals and
facilities.
